## Overview

AGI-adjacent reasoning refers to systems that express **general-capability behavior** without claiming:

- human equivalence  
- subjective experience  
- identity, intent, or autonomy  
- simulated human cognition  

These systems achieve cross-domain competence through **architecture, structural constraints, and compositional scaling**, not through human psychological imitation.

**Emergent reasoning** describes capabilities that arise from **interactions within the systemâ€™s structure**, not from explicit training labels.  
These behaviors:

- appear when components are composed or scaled  
- cannot be localized to a single parameter or dataset  
- are not programmed directly  
- exceed narrow optimization  

Emergence is a *property of architecture*, not a feature of human likeness.
